# Business Plan

## SWOT Analysis

### Strengths

- Support for in-person gameplay;
- Innovative hybrid approach;
- Easily expandable architecture.

### Weaknesses

- Dependence on lighting conditions and camera angles;
- Android-only frontend.

### Opportunities

- Partnerships with associations and cultural events;
- Tournament organisation support;
- Physical kit (tripod) as a complementary product;
- Reuse of computer vision for other card games.

### Threats

- High computational costs;
- Resistance from traditional users;
- Changes in user habits.

---

## TOWS Matrix

### Strengths × Opportunities

- Expand to other card games;
- Organise technologically supported tournaments;
- Develop partnerships with cultural institutions.

### Strengths × Threats

- Physical gameplay support reduces resistance from traditional players;
- Flexible architecture enables adaptation to changing markets.

### Weaknesses × Opportunities

- Physical kits may reduce camera-related limitations.

### Weaknesses × Threats

- Detection limitations combined with user resistance may hinder adoption.

---

## PESTEL Analysis

### Political

- Support for preserving traditional culture;
- Incentives for digital innovation.

### Economic

- Low implementation costs due to open-source technologies;
- Hardware limitations may exclude some users.

### Social

- Sueca has strong cultural relevance;
- Existing familiarity may facilitate adoption.

### Technological

- Rapid evolution of AI may require continuous updates;
- Camera quality affects detection reliability.

### Environmental

- Video processing increases energy consumption;
- Agent training contributes to carbon emissions;
- Hybrid and virtual modes may reduce travel-related emissions.

### Legal

- AI regulations continue to evolve;
- Ongoing compliance monitoring is required.
